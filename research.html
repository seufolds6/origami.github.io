Research Publications




The American Physical Society


We studied an origami spring structure mechanically. The spring structure is useful in robotics applications because it is structurally stable at every point as it is extended. We predicted the ideal maximum height and formulated a relationship between the angle of rotation and the spring height.

See the abstract for the conference.


Advanced Intelligent Systems


We designed and prototyped a thin‐film‐based flexible robot. This robot uses mechanical instability and electrostatic force actuation for locomotion. We added bi-stable origami-inspired legs so that it can move either both forward or backward when the legs switch between the two mechanically stable states.

Visit the paper and the news article.


The National Museum of Mathematics


We analyzed an origami spring structure mathematically. We obtained a Poisson’s ratio of the spring structure, showing it is not constant and is scalable with respect to different geometric parameters.
